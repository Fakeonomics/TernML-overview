How It Works

Train. Quantize. Deploy.
Three steps, one framework.

A pipeline that turns standard PyTorch or TensorFlow models into ultra-efficient ternary networks for any microcontroller.

01

Train with QAT

Define your architecture with standard layers. TernML's quantization-aware training learns ternary weight distributions while backpropagating through discrete constraints — no custom ops needed.

PyTorch • TensorFlow
02

Quantize to {-1,0,+1}

Trained float weights convert to ternary values using a threshold-aware scheme. The result: 1.58 bits per parameter with ~49% natural sparsity — zeros cost nothing in compute or storage.

1.58 bit • 8× smaller
03

Deploy Anywhere

Export a 15 KB binary. Flash to any Cortex-M0+, ESP32, RISC-V, or MIK32 Amur. No DSP, no FPU, no OS — just bare-metal inference in milliseconds.

About

Built for the edge.‌

TernML is an independent research project focused on bringing neural network inference to the cheapest microcontrollers.

TernML was developed from the conviction that neural networks should not require expensive GPUs or specialized hardware. The core discovery: ternary quantization acts as a regularizer during training, improving generalization over the float baseline.

“Discrete ternary weights act as a regularizer during training, naturally pruning noise while preserving signal.” — Regularization-by-Quantization Effect

The result: models that don't just compress well — they generalize better. This is TernML’s core insight.
