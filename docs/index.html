How It Works

Train normally.
Quantize once. Deploy anywhere.

A three-step pipeline that turns standard PyTorch/TensorFlow models into ultra-efficient ternary networks.

1

Train

Define your architecture using standard layers. TernML's quantization-aware training automatically learns ternary weight distributions while backpropagating through discrete constraints.

2

Quantize

The trained float weights are converted to {-1,0,+1} using a threshold-aware scheme. The result: 1.58 bit per parameter with natural sparsity — zeros are free.

3

Deploy

Export to a 15 KB binary. Flash onto any Cortex-M0+, ESP32, RISC-V, or MIK32 Amur. No DSP, no FPU, no OS — just bare-metal inference with millisecond latency.

About

Built by an independent researcher.

TernML is the work of a single developer driven by a simple belief: intelligence should run on the cheapest hardware.

Fakeonomics started building TernML at 17 years old, after completing 9th grade — driven by the conviction that neural networks should not require expensive GPUs or specialized hardware. Entirely self-taught, they developed a novel ternary quantization pipeline that, counterintuitively, improves accuracy over the float baseline.

The core insight: discrete ternary weights act as a regularizer during training, naturally pruning noise while preserving signal. This regularization-by-quantization effect means TernML models don't just compress well — they generalize better.
